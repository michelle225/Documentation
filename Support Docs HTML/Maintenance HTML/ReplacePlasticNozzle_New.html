Removing the Plastic Nozzle

Before you begin the process of replacing your plastic nozzle please note that your Mark Two printer has a built in utility which will walk you through the process of replacing your nozzle step by step.

You will need the following tools to remove the plastic nozzle on your printer:

  • 7mm Torque Driver

To remove the plastic nozzle on your Mark Two printer:

  1. Press the menu button in the upper right corner of your printer's LCD touch screen.



  2. Select "Utilities" from the list of menu options.



  3. Select "Advanced Utilities" from the list of menu option.



  4. Select "Replace Nylon Nozzle" to begin the nylon nozzle replacement walkthrough.



  5. Use the 7mm torque driver to loosen the plastic nozzle once it is heated.
    Note: Be sure the torque driver has solid contact at all points on the nozzle. Failure to do so may result in your plastic nozzle being stripped.



  6. Follow the on-screen prompts to cool down the print head.



  7. Once the print head is cool, press the "Next" button.



  8. Unscrew and remove the plastic nozzle from the print head.
    Note: This step can be done with your hands or with the torque driver.

Replacing the Plastic Nozzle

You will need the following tools to remove the plastic nozzle on your printer:

  • 7mm Torque Driver
  • Small Tube of Anti-Seize
  • Replacement Plastic Nozzle

To replace the plastic nozzle on your Mark Two printer:

  1. Apply a small amount of Anti-Seize to the end of the threads of the new nozzle.
    Note: Be sure not to get any of the Anti-Seize in the nozzle.
  2. Thread the new nozzle into the print head until it is finger tight.
  3. Unscrew the new nozzle and then thread it into the print head again. This helps to disperse the Anti-Seize over all of the the threads.



  4. Press the "Next" button on the touch screen to reheat the print head.



  5. When the print head reaches temperature, use the torque driver to tighten the nozzle until it clicks.
    Note: Be sure the torque driver has solid contact at all points on the nozzle. Failure to do so may result in your plastic nozzle being stripped.

    <

  6. Once complete, run the "Load Nylon" routine.

You printer is now ready for use. Be careful not to touch the metal on your torque driver directly after running this routine as it will be extremely hot.
