Removing the Print Head

Occassionally a print head may become damaged or may need to be replaced. Before doing any maintenance on the print head, please unload any nylon or fiber that is in the printer. For more information about unloading materials, please see Unloading Materials.

To remove the print head from your Mark Two printer:

  1. Turn your printer off.
  2. Remove the thumbscrews connecting both Bowden Tubes to the print head. Allow the tubes to hang freely.



  3. Slide the white Axon cable clip away from the print head and the circuit board.
  4. Gently squeeze the tabs at the top and bottom of the Axon cable connector to release it from the board. Allow the cable to hang freely.
  5. Use a 2.5mm Allen wrench to loosen and remove the two bolts connecting the print head to the print head base.



  6. Carefully pull the print head off of the print head base. Be careful not to damage the pegs that the print head sits on.

Replacing the Print Head

To replace the print head on your Mark Two printer:

  1. Carefully place the new print head onto the print head base. Be careful to align the pegs on the print head base with the holes on the print head so that the pegs are not damaged.





  2. Use a 2.5mm Allen wrench to tighten the two bolts that connect the print head to the print head base.



  3. Reconnect the Axon cable and the Axon cable clip. Be careful not to break the cable, clip, or board but also be sure the cable is secure.
  4. Replace the Bowden Tubes for both materials and tighten the thumb screws into the print head.
  5. Turn your printer on.
  6. Reload plastic and fiber into your printer.
  7. Plug the provded USB key into your printer.
  8. Select "USB/On Board Storage" from the menu in the upper right corner of your printer's LCD touch screen.
  9. Select the file from the USB key and press "Print". This will update the offsets for the print head.

Your print head is now installed and ready to print.
