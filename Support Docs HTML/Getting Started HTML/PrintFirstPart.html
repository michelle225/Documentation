Importing STLs Into Eiger

To import an STL file into Eiger:

  1. Sign in to your Eiger account
  2. Click on the "Import STL" button in the Library Header or the "Import Icon" in the Navbar.



  3. Select a .STL file to import from your computer.

  4. Use the "X", "Y", and "Z" fields under the "Part Settings" panel to change the orientation of your part.



    Note: If your part imports in an orientation that will not fit on the print bed, the part will turn red. To fix this, adjust the rotation of the part so that it does fit.



  5. Use the "Scale" box to change the proportionate size of the part.



    Note: If your part is too small or too large, try using the "Original Units" field under "Advanced Settings" to change the units on the scale.



  6. Toggle the "Use Fiber" button if you want your part to be printed with fiber. For more information about printing with fiber, see Adding Fiber to a Part



  7. Once your part is configured click Save. The part will begin to slice. Once it is finished you will be able to print your part or edit it further.


Your part will now show up in your Library. You can return and print or edit it at any time.

Printing Parts

To print your part:

  1. Locate your part in Eiger by either:
    • Navigating to the library page and selecting the part you want to print
    • Searching for the part you want to print and then selecting it

  2. Once the part has loaded, click "Print". This will take you to the Build Page.



  3. The build page is where you can configure how your print. Here you can:

    • Click and drag your part to position it on the build platform.
    • Click on the "X" next to a part's name to remove it from the build.
    • Add more parts to your build by selecting a Part from the dropdown in "Add Part" and then clicking "Add"



      Note: New Parts are added to the center of the build plate. You have to drag them to a new position on the print bed so that parts do not collide.

Once all the parts in your build have been configured you can print your parts by following the instructions below.

Print to a Connected Mark Two

If you have a Mark Two that is registered with your account, connected to the internet and is ready to print you can select it from the "Select Printer" dropdown. Once a printer has been selected you can click the "Print" button which will generate the print and send it to your Mark Two.

Print to an Offline Mark Two

If your Mark Two is not connected to the internet click the "Download" button. Eiger will generate and download a .mfp file for you to use offline. Simply copy this file onto a USB drive and insert it into the back of your Mark Two. Navigate to the Settings Menu and select "Print From USB". Then select your print from the list of available prints.

Saving Builds

Prints that contain more than one part (or copy of a part) are called "Builds". Once more than one part has been added to a print a button will appear that says "Save as Build". If you think you will print this set of parts again save it as a build for easy access.
