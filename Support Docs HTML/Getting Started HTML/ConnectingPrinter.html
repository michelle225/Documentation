Goals:

  • Ensure your printer is ready to be connected
  • Connect your computer to your printer via:
    1. Wifi (via your LAN) - wirelessly print a part directly from within Eiger by selecting/printing to your online printer.
    2. Ethernet (via your LAN) - print a part directly from within Eiger by selecting/printing to your online printer via wired connection.
    3. Print from USB - Printing an mfp file from a USB (FAT32) thumb drive inserted into the USB port located on the back of the printer. 
    4. Print from on-board memory - Printing an mfp file from the printer's on-board memory/storage.  

 

 

Before You Get Started

If you powered up your Mark Two Composite 3D Printer™ before registering your machine, or for Eiger, you will need to cycle the power (turn off and on again) before your printer will be connected to your account.

An mfp file can be created / exported from Eiger by selecting "Export Build" as the printer selection.  You can transfer the created mfp file to the printer's on-board memory/storage via a USB cable connected to your PC.

You cannot transfer mfp files to the printer's internal memory/storage via a USB (FAT32) thumb drive - you can only print an mfp file from the USB (FAT32) thumb drive.

You cannot print directly to the Mark Two 3D printer via the USB cable (or Ethernet cable) from your PC (no printer drivers are available) - you can only transfer mfp files to the printer's internal memory/storage via the USB cable.

Connect via Wi-Fi

To connect to your printer via WiFi:

  1. On the Mark Two touchscreen, touch the "Wi-Fi" icon in the upper right corner.



    Note: If you are offline, the "Offline" icon will be highlighted. If you are connected, the type of connectivity you are using will be highlighted.

  2. To connect to your wireless network, press the "Wi-Fi" icon; it will highlight in blue.



  3. Select a network to join from the dropdown menu.
  4. You may be prompted to enter a password or network key once your network is selected. If prompted, enter the password and select "Save".



    Note: The next screen will show that your network is seeking Eiger, this should only take a few moments.

  5. Once your network has found Eiger, the printer will show you that it has successfully connected and is online.

Connect via Ethernet (LAN)

To connect to your printer via ethernet

  1. Plug your ethernet cable into the ethernet port.
    Note: Make sure your LAN (Local Area Network) uses DHCP.
  2. Reboot your Mark Two.
  3. Once your Mark Two reboots you should see the Ethernet Symbol in the upper right corner of the screen.

Print from USB

To print mfp files from a USB thumb drive:

  1. Plug in your USB thumb drive to the port on back of printer
  2. Select the menu button in the upper right corner of the LCD touch screen.
  3. Select "USB/Onboard Storage" from the menu options.
  4. Select "Print from USB" from the menu options.
  5. Select the desired mfp file to print.

Print from on-board storage

To print mfp files saved to on-board storage:

  1. Plug a USB cable into the printer and your computer.
  2. Transfer files from your computer to the printer's on-board storage.
  3. Select the menu button in the upper right corner of the LCD touch screen.
  4. Select "Onboard Storage" from the menu options.
  5. Select the desired mfp file to print.
