Desiging for 3D Printing with Plastic

3D printing lets you break most of the rules in traditional manufacturing processes, but there are a few guidelines that will help you optimize parts for this process.

To have the most success printing a part:

  • Orient the part so that there is a flat base to provide good adhesion to the print bed.
  • Use chamfers to connect overhangs to the base of the part
  • Use default infill settings. For more information about infill settings, see Infill Settings
  • Use fillets on the top of a part for a smooth finish.

Printing Teardrops

To print a teardrop shape:

  • Use a flat base for good bed adhesion
  • Use a chamfer for the overhang up to 45 degrees
  • Use a full fillet for the top

Example of a teardrop shaped part:

  • The external chamfer is set to 20 degrees from vertical. Up to 45 degrees would print fine but, in this case, a wider angle would affect the airflow around the part.
  • The internal wire channel has a 45 degree chamfer on top to close the cavity without supports.
  • The chamfer on the bottom of the wire channel will add wall thickness to the part and increase strength.

Infill Settings

Eiger gives you several options for filling a part you are printing.

  • Default/Triangular: A fill pattern that increases speed capabilities of the printer while still making a strong part.
  • Hexagonal: A fill pattern made up of larger hexagons that will result in a light part but with somewhat reduced strength.
  • Rectangular: A fill pattern that will result in your part being close to 100% filled with solid plastic.
