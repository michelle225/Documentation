Creating Strong Parts

When working with composite materials, a sandwich panel is a load-distribution device that is similar to an I-Beam. In a sandwich panel, the top and bottom layers are made of fiber or some other strong material; the middle is made of nylon or another light material. In this design, the majority of the load is resisted by the carbon. The interior plastic simply transfers the sheer stress between the top and bottom layers.

Printing a Part with Sandwich Panels

To print a part using the sandwich panel fiber layout:

  1. Open Eiger and import your STL file.



  2. Toggle the "Use Fiber" button in the "Part Settings" panel on the right side of the screen.
    Note: If the toggle button is moved to the right and the oval is green, fiber is already being used in the part.

     

  3. Configure settings for printing with fiber in the "Part Settings" panel.



  4. Check that fiber settings are correct in the "Internal View" screen. Fiber layers will be visible on the part as either blue or yellow lines.





  5. To add or remove layers, or to change the fiber layout, see "Manual Fiber Routing"

Creating Flexible Parts

If you want a part to be more flexible or do not need it to have strong load bearing capabilities, you can choose to put fiber just in the middle of a part. This can save material, if you choose to use fewer fiber layers in the middle than you would have had from the top and bottom.

To set up a print with fiber in the middle:

  1. Open Eiger and import your STL file



  2. Toggle the "Use Fiber" button in the "Part Settings" panel on the right side of the screen.
    Note: If the toggle button is moved to the right and the oval is green, fiber is already being used in the part.

     

  3. Configure settings for printing with fiber in the "Part Settings" panel.



  4. Click the "Internal View" button to bring up the view of the layers of the part.
  5. In the 3D View, click on the carbon layers at the bottom and press the "F" key on your keyboard to remove them.



  6. Click the "2D" button near the top right of the screen to switch the view to that of individual layers
  7. Find the central layer of your part by clicking on the bar at the bottom of the screen or by using the arrow keys.
  8. Use the "F" key on your keyboard to add fiber to the desired number of central layers for your part.
    Note: Remember that the more fiber you put in the center of your part, the less flexible it will be.

Printing Flat Parts

For parts that are flat or that have parallel top and bottom plates, the use of sandwich panels is a good design choice. The fiber at the top and bottom of the part will keep the part rigid and sturdy, whlie the plastic in the center will keep the part light and cost effective. In addition to the top and bototm fiber plates, depending on the strength or size of the part, it may be useful to add another fiber plate to the center of the part. The central fiber plate will help promote shape and integrity of the part when subjected to loads or stress.

To set up a part to print flat:
