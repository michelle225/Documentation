These instructions will walk you through the installation of your nylon shield. The shield will work for Mark Two print heads with both shrouds, as depicted here.

 

 

Kit items:

 

Tools needed:

  • Plastic nozzle torque wrench
  • Fiber nozzle wrench
  • 2mm and 2.5mm hex wrenches
  • tweezers (optional to assist with aligning components)

 

Shaping the flat sheet

  1. Make sure you have the top side facing up. The top side has the larger diagonal cut on the right side.

 

2. Fold the lower 3 tabs in all the way and press hard on the folds. When released, the flaps should be almost perpendicular to the sheet. (see picture below for reference)

3. Curl the longer section up. 

 

Installing shield on print head, while print head is on printer

  1. Heat up the print head to 275°C and remove nylon nozzle.
  2. Remove fiber nozzle.
  3. Move print head to the middle of the printer for easier access.
  4. Turn OFF printer and let it cool.
  5. Remove the white Axon cable clip by pulling it away from the print head.
  6. Remove Axon cable by pinching the black tabs on the connector and pulling.

 

7. Remove the 2 PCB screws using the 2 mm hex wrench and discard them. (some PCB screws may require a 2.5 mm hex.)

 

8. Put the shield washer on the nylon nozzle.

 

9. With the shield's 3 tabs facing up, slip the shield on the bottom of the print head. Make sure each of the tabs are tucked in under the sheet metal and the longer section is behind the PCB. You may have to push the white insulation up into the print head.

 

10. Apply anti-seize to the new fiber nozzle and install with PTFE tube and nylon nozzle with the shield washer loosely to align the shield.

 

11. Make sure the fiber nozzle is sitting flush to the heater block and that it is not pinching the shield. Then tighten the fiber nozzle, ensuring there are no open gaps.

 

12. Use the 2.5 mm hex wrench to install the replacement screws with the retaining washers in the PCB holes to constrain the shield.

IMPORTANT: Push the shield up while tightening the screws to prevent the shield from sagging and interfering with prints.

13. Reinstall Axon cable and clip
14. Turn on printer
15. Heat up nylon nozzle to 275°C and torque it in
16. Load nylon and fiber to check that the nozzles' alignment is good
17. Relevel bed
18. Discard any remaining fiber nozzles as they are not compatible with the new shield.
