Problem:

  • Fiber is not printing from the nozzle

 

Clearing the Fiber Nozzle

p>From time to time the fiber nozzle on your Mark Two printer can occassionaly get clogged. This is especially common after a failed print.

In the event that your fiber nozzle appears to be clogged, remove the nozzle and check to see if the PTFE tube is clogged. If it is clogged, replace the PTFE tube and reinstall the nozzle. This will fix 90% of clogged nozzle issues. If the PTFE tube is not clogged, continue to the steps below. For more information about removing and replacing the nozzle or PTFE tube, please see Replacing the Mark Two Fiber Nozzle.

To clear out the fiber nozzle on your Mark Two printer:

  1. Carefully unscrew the Fiber Bowden Adapter from the top of the printhead.
  2. Select "Utilities" from the menu in the upper right corner of your printer's touch screen.



  3. Select "Temperature Control" from the list of menu options.



  4. Select "Heat" and then "Both" to heat up both nozzles.





  5. When the nozzles are up to temperature, loosen the thumb screen for the Fiber Bowden Tube and pull the tube adapter free of the print head.
    Be careful not to touch the fiber as it will be very hot.
  6. Using the tweezers provided, pull the fiber free of the Bowden tube adapter and discard.
  7. Select "Utilities" from the menu in the upper right corner of your printer's touch screen.



  8. Select "Temperature Control" from the list of menu options. Set both nozzles to cool down.



  9. Select "Cool" then "Both" to cool both nozzles.





  10. Look down through the fiber nozzle from the top; if it is clear all the way through, reattach the Fiber Bowden Tube adapter to the printhead.
  11. If the nozzle is not visibly clear, remove the fiber nozzle and inspect the disposable fiber tube tip.  If it is clogged, install a new fiber tube tip.  Before installing the new fiber tube tip, make sure there is no other blockage in the fiber nozzle.  If there is other blockage, clean or replace the nozzle.
    Note: For more information, see Replacing the Mark Two Fiber Nozzle.
  12. Reattach the Fiber Bowden Tube adapter.
  13. Run the Load Fiber routine from the printer touchscreen to confirm that the fiber feed system is running correctly.
